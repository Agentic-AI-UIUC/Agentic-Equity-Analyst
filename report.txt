Below is a concise, research‑style 2026 outlook on Costco (COST). All data and price/technical reference points are as of my knowledge cutoff in October 2024; any 2025–26 figures are scenario-based estimates, not forecasts.

---

## 1. Company & Industry Context

**Business model**

- Membership-based warehouse club focused on value and limited SKU “treasure hunt” assortment.
- Core profit engine is **membership fees**, not merchandise margin; this structurally drives high loyalty, strong pricing power, and defensiveness.
- Revenue mix: merchandise (core food, consumables, hardlines, softlines), gasoline, and membership fees; growing contribution from services and e‑commerce.

**Industry position**

- Global warehouse club leader; key competitors:
  - **Sam’s Club** (WMT), **BJ’s Wholesale (BJ)** in warehouse clubs.
  - **Walmart (WMT), Target (TGT), Kroger** and large grocers in broader retail.
- Structural advantages:
  - Scale and purchasing leverage.
  - Tight cost structure and low SG&A.
  - Extremely high renewal rates and traffic density.
- Sector backdrop into 2026:
  - Mature U.S. retail but continued share gains for value formats.
  - Ongoing shift toward club/value and private label in a slower, more inflation‑normalized environment.

---

## 2. Historical Financial Performance (through FY24, approximate)

**Revenue & growth**

- FY19–FY24: high single‑ to low double‑digit **CAGR in net sales** (helped by unit growth, ticket growth, and some inflation tailwind in FY21–23).
- **Membership fee income** growing mid‑ to high‑single digits, consistently outpacing unit growth (ARPU & mix).

**Margins**

- Operating margin structurally low (~3% range) but **very stable**:
  - Gross margin low vs peers by design; merchandising margin sacrificed to reinforce value perception.
  - SG&A discipline and scale offsets wage and logistics pressure over time.
- Membership income effectively subsidizes merchandise margins, allowing Costco to underprice peers while preserving ROIC.

**Member metrics**

- **Renewal rates**: typically ~90%+ in the U.S. & Canada and high‑80s globally.
- **Paid household and cardholder count**: steady mid‑single‑digit growth driven by:
  - New clubs.
  - Penetration of existing catchment areas.
  - Gradual mix shift to higher‑priced Executive memberships.

**Unit growth / store footprint**

- Net new warehouse openings: typically **~20–30+ per year**, accelerating in international (Asia, Europe).
- Strong new‑club productivity and high returns on invested capital; few closures.

**Free cash flow (FCF)**

- Strong FCF generation:
  - Higher working‑capital efficiency vs many retailers (membership fees are upfront cash).
  - Capex intensity rising with international expansion and logistics/IT but covered comfortably by operating cash flow.

**Balance sheet**

- Very conservative; modest net debt (often net cash or near net-cash position).
- Substantial capacity for special dividends or stepped‑up capex without stressing balance sheet.

**Capital returns**

- **Dividend**: steady annual increases; modest yield but strong growth track.
- **Special dividends**: periodic, sizable distributions when cash accumulates.
- **Share repurchases**: opportunistic rather than aggressive; capital allocation tilted to growth capex + dividends.

## 3. Forward-looking Fundamentals: 2026 Outlook

**Key structural drivers**

- **Unit expansion**:
  - Continued U.S. infill but increasingly international (Asia/Europe and potentially Latin America).
  - High-return new clubs remain the most important EPS driver.
- **Membership economics**:
  - Ongoing growth in paid members and mix shift to higher-tier Executive plan.
  - Potential **membership fee increase** by 2026 (historically every ~5–6 years) is a material profit lever.
- **Merchandise & channel mix**:
  - Food/consumables resilient; discretionary/hardlines more cyclical.
  - Gradual e‑commerce and services growth (optical, pharmacy, travel, etc.) as incremental profit drivers.
- **Operating leverage vs wage/logistics**:
  - Wage inflation and logistics costs are a headwind, but scale and productivity improvements provide some offset.

### Scenario framework to FY26 (directional, approximate)

Assuming FY24 as a base and looking two fiscal years forward:

#### Base case (probability: central)

- **Revenue growth FY24–26**:
  - Net sales CAGR: **~6–8%**.
  - Drivers: ~3–4% from unit growth, ~2–3% from comps/ticket, modest benefit from mix.
- **Operating margin**:
  - Roughly **stable in the low‑3% range**.
  - Membership fee increase (if enacted by FY25–26) offsets wage and occupancy pressure.
- **EPS growth**:
  - **High single‑ to low double‑digit** annual EPS growth (around 8–12%).
  - Contribution from unit growth, membership income, and modest buybacks.
- **FCF**:
  - FCF grows roughly in line with or slightly above EPS as capex scales but remains disciplined.

#### Bull case (probability: lower, but plausible)

- **Revenue growth FY24–26**:
  - Net sales CAGR: **~9–11%**.
  - Stronger international performance; robust traffic; stable/mildly positive real pricing.
- **Operating margin**:
  - Moves modestly **above historical band** (e.g., +20–40 bps vs base), helped by:
    - Favorable merchandising mix and scale.
    - Higher membership fee income (including fee hike).
    - Operating leverage in SG&A and logistics.
- **EPS growth**:
  - **Low‑ to mid‑teens** CAGR.
- **FCF**:
  - Outgrows revenue on leverage and high incremental ROIC; room for another sizable special dividend and/or increased buybacks.

#### Bear case (probability: meaningful but not central)

- **Revenue growth FY24–26**:
  - Net sales CAGR: **~3–5%**.
  - Macro slowdown and weaker discretionary spend; slower unit expansion in some markets.
- **Operating margin**:
  - **Down modestly** (e.g., -20–40 bps vs base), driven by:
    - Wage/benefit inflation outpacing productivity.
    - Promotional activity in more competitive markets.
- **EPS growth**:
  - **Low‑single‑digit** or flat EPS CAGR; some downside risk in a deeper recession.
- **FCF**:
  - Still positive and solid, but less capacity for special dividends; capex may be re‑phased.

## 4. Valuation: Current Positioning & 2026 Framework

(Approximate multiples as of late 2024; exact levels may vary.)

**Current snapshot (late 2024):**

- **P/E (NTM)**: ~**38–42x**.
- **EV/EBITDA (NTM)**: ~**20–22x**.
- **FCF yield**: typically **~2–3%** on normalized FCF.
- Market assigns a **quality + growth + defensiveness premium**.

**Historical context**

- Costco has **long traded at a premium**:
  - Typical historical P/E band: ~25–35x, with peaks higher during periods of elevated confidence or low rates.
  - EV/EBITDA long‑run band often low‑ to mid‑teens, with a premium phase in recent years.
- Today’s multiples sit **toward the upper end** of its historical range, particularly vs pre‑COVID norms.

**Peer comparison (approximate NTM multiples, late 2024)**

- **Walmart (WMT)**:
  - P/E: ~22–25x.
  - EV/EBITDA: mid‑teens.
- **Target (TGT)**:
  - P/E: high‑teens to low‑20s (cyclical).
  - EV/EBITDA: low‑teens.
- **BJ’s Wholesale (BJ)**:
  - P/E: low‑ to mid‑20s.
  - EV/EBITDA: low‑ to mid‑teens.

Costco’s premium vs peers is large but structurally persistent, reflecting:

- Higher traffic and loyalty.
- Superior renewal economics.
- Very strong balance sheet and FCF quality.

### 2026 valuation framework (illustrative)

Assume FY26 EPS under scenarios:

- **Base**: EPS grows high single/low double digit → index to 100 in FY24 → **~120–125** by FY26.
- **Bull**: EPS grows low/mid‑teens → **~130–140** by FY26.
- **Bear**: EPS flat to low‑single‑digit → **~105–110** by FY26.

Apply P/E multiples:

1. **Base case valuation (FY26)**
   - Multiple range: **32–38x** (slight de‑rating vs peak, but still premium).
   - Implied price vs FY24 base:
     - EPS +20–25% × P/E 32–38x → **rough price upside in the low‑ to mid‑teens** from current if the multiple settles toward the lower end, and **20–30% upside** if the multiple holds near the high end.

2. **Bull case valuation (FY26)**
   - Multiple: **38–42x** (sustained premium on higher confidence/growth).
   - EPS +30–40% → **potential upside ~30–45%** vs current levels.

3. **Bear case valuation (FY26)**
   - Multiple: **24–30x** (re‑rating closer to a high‑quality retailer rather than a unique compounder).
   - EPS +5–10% or flattish → **downside ~15–30%** vs current, depending on severity of de‑rating.

**Verdict on valuation**

- On standard metrics, Costco screens as **expensive vs its history and vs peers**, but the premium is supported by:
  - High visibility into growth (unit expansion, membership).
  - Robust balance sheet and FCF.
  - Defensive characteristics in downturns.
- For **new capital**, the margin of safety is modest at current levels; risk/reward is skewed by starting valuation.

## 5. Key Risks & Catalysts Through 2026

**Macro & consumer**

- Slower real wage growth or recession could pressure discretionary categories, though consumables are defensive.
- Inflation normalization or deflation can reduce nominal comp growth, particularly gasoline and some food categories.

**Competitive dynamics**

- Intensifying price investment from:
  - Walmart/Sam’s (core competitor).
  - Discount grocers and dollar stores in lower‑income segments.
- E‑commerce/omni‑channel:
  - Pressure from Amazon and others could limit non‑food/hardlines growth.

**Membership dynamics**

- Any **unexpected slowdown** in:
  - New members per club.
  - Renewal rates (particularly in newer international markets).
  - Executive upgrade rate.
- Delayed or mishandled **membership fee increase** (e.g., poor timing in a soft macro environment) could impact sentiment, though historically hikes have been well absorbed.

**Cost & wage pressure**

- Higher labor, logistics, and rent/occupancy:
  - Could squeeze SG&A if productivity doesn’t keep up.
  - Costco’s philosophy is to pay relatively well; this can compress margins if not offset by scale and price optimization.

**Execution & expansion risk**

- Large international projects (e.g., rapid entry into new markets) may carry higher execution risk.
- Supply chain disruptions (e.g., geopolitical events, shipping costs) affecting inventory or margins.

**Regulatory & FX**

- FX volatility impacting reported revenue and earnings from non‑U.S. operations.
- Regulatory issues around labor, trade, data, or operations in certain countries.

**Key positive catalysts (2024–26)**

- **Membership fee increase** announcement and implementation.
- Strong new market launches (especially in Asia/Europe).
- Evidence of sustained high‑teens EPS growth (bullish re‑rating or multiple support).
- Additional **special dividends** or acceleration in capital returns.

## 6. Technical Analysis (through Oct 2024)

Note: Exact indicator values can move meaningfully day to day; below is the structural picture as of late 2024.

### Trend & moving averages (365/200/50‑day)

- **Price vs MAs**:
  - Spot price in late 2024 sits **above the 50‑day and 200‑day MAs**, and meaningfully above any longer‑term (e.g., ~365‑day) average.
- **MA alignment**:
  - **50‑day > 200‑day > 365‑day (or ≈365‑day)**: classic **bullish trend structure**.
  - No major “death cross”; instead, prior or ongoing “golden cross” environment.

**Trend regime:** **Clear uptrend** on intermediate and long‑term horizons.

### RSI (momentum / overbought conditions)

- Daily **RSI in the 70+ zone** on recent rallies, indicating **overbought** conditions.
- Historically, Costco can remain overbought for some time in strong trends, but:
  - Short‑term pullbacks or consolidations often follow extended stays >70.
  - Negative RSI divergence (if price makes new highs while RSI peaks lower) would be an early signal of trend fatigue.

### ATR (volatility / market speed)

- **ATR as a % of price** around **1.5–2.0%**:
  - Indicates **moderate realized volatility**: not low like a bond proxy, not high like a small cap.
  - Compared to its own history, this is consistent with a **steady, trending environment** rather than crisis or shock.
- “Market speed”:
  - Price is trending higher with **reasonable, not extreme, daily ranges**, suggestive of a **controlled advance** rather than euphoric blow‑off.

### Support, resistance, and structure

- **Support zones**:
  - Recent breakout levels (prior highs) now act as first support.
  - 50‑day MA: first dynamic support in shallow pullbacks.
  - 200‑day MA: key support in deeper corrections; historically, Costco tends to respect this in bull phases.
- **Resistance**:
  - Current or recent all‑time highs.
  - Any failure to hold breakouts (false breakout) plus RSI divergence would be an early warning sign.

### Indicator confirmation vs divergence

- **Confirmation**:
  - Price > 50‑ and 200‑day MAs, upward‑sloping MAs, and an RSI in the 60–75 region collectively confirm a **bull trend**.
  - Positive momentum: trend and RSI mostly agree with price action.
- **Potential divergence**:
  - If RSI begins making lower highs while price pushes to new highs, that would indicate **waning momentum**.
  - If ATR starts rising sharply with choppy candles (larger intraday ranges) near highs, that could signal a **distribution phase** rather than healthy accumulation.

Net technical message (late 2024):

- **Intermediate/long‑term trend: bullish.**
- **Short‑term condition: overbought, vulnerable to mean‑reversion or consolidation.**
- Volatility is moderate; the tape does not suggest panic, but upside is not “cheap” on a tactical basis.

## 7. Integrated 12–24 Month View (into 2026)

### Synthesis: Fundamentals + Valuation + Technicals

- **Fundamentals**:
  - High‑quality, defensive compounder with durable growth levers (units + membership).
  - Visibility is relatively high; true cyclical downside is modest vs typical discretionary retailers.
- **Valuation**:
  - Trading at a **full to rich premium** vs both its own longer‑term history and large‑cap retail peers.
  - The multiple assumes continued execution with limited macro or competitive shocks.
- **Technicals**:
  - Strong uptrend, supportive for longer‑term bulls.
  - Near‑term overbought conditions raise the risk of **tactical pullbacks**.

### 12–24 month risk/reward (from late 2024 levels)

Indicative price path outcomes (broad ranges, not price targets):

- **Base case (most likely)**:
  - EPS grows high single/low double digit; P/E moderates slightly but remains premium.
  - **Upside: ~10–20%** over 12–24 months (mid‑teens central band).
  - Total return modestly enhanced by dividend and potential special distributions.

- **Bull case**:
  - Strong macro + accelerated international; membership fee hike executed smoothly; margins hold or expand modestly; sentiment stays strong, supporting a high multiple.
  - **Upside: ~25–40%** if both EPS and multiple expand/hold.

- **Bear case**:
  - Macro slowdown, wage and cost pressures, or sentiment shift lead to multiple compression toward high‑quality retail norms.
  - EPS still generally positive over the cycle but below expectations.
  - **Downside: ~15–30%**, mainly via re‑rating; fundamentals would likely remain intact but repriced.

### Overall stance (2024 → 2026)

- **Fundamental stance**: Positive/constructive. Costco remains one of the highest‑quality business models in global retail with attractive long‑term compounding prospects.
- **Valuation stance**: **Cautious**. Entry point is not obviously attractive; prospective returns rely more on earnings growth than further multiple expansion.
- **Technical stance**: Bullish intermediate term, but **tactically stretched**, suggesting patience may be rewarded (e.g., adding on pullbacks toward key support or mean reversion in RSI).

**Integrated view:**  
- **12–24 month rating bias: Neutral to moderately bullish.**
  - Attractive long‑term compounder, but current valuation and overbought technicals limit near‑term upside vs downside.
  - For investors already long, Costco remains a **hold/underweight‑trim on strength** rather than an outright exit.
  - For new capital, a **staggered or buy‑on‑weakness approach** into 2025–26 offers a better balance between Costco’s excellent fundamentals and its premium starting valuation.
